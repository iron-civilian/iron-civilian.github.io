We study motility-induced phase separation behavior of RTPs in 2D. We look at the percolation properties of clusters formed and characterize the critical behavior. We obeserve a continuous variation in exponents which we explain through the Super-universality Hypothesis. (arxiv) (code)

We study a semi-conserved version of the Ashkin-Teller Model. The non-conserved counterpart is known to exhibit a magnetic and polarization transition at the same critical point, whose location is a function of the inter-layer coupling λ. We study magnetic and percolation transition in the conserved one and compare the critical behavior with the non-conserved version. (code)

We study the effect of non-reciprocity in the classical Ising Model. We modify the ordinary Ising model to incorporate non-reciprocal interactions. We study several cases present in the literature and show that despite the novel phase behavior induced by non-reciprocity, it fails to modify the critical behavior of the magnetization transition. (code)
